## Issue Summary

Issue 10 is the assembly chapter. The Ark, identified in Issue 07 and partially begun (1 component), must now be made whole. Three remaining components are scattered across the archive's accessible records of T5's path. The player navigates three short shard panels to recover each component, then performs the full Ark Assembly puzzle.

After assembly, the Ark becomes an active tool — its five abilities now accessible. The first ability test (E2 Carry Constraint) is required: the Ark must be carried into specific position using the dual-pole mechanic, navigating obstacles in the T5 passage toward Giza.

The Identity Node (Akhenaten/Moses) resolves here. The player discovers what the exile carried — the first Ark component was with him. The two-figure node collapses into a single confirmed truth.

## Page 5 — "Ark Assembly" (Puzzle: E1 Full)

**Format**: Full-page puzzle — the most important assembly in the game

**Puzzle type**: E1 Ark Assembly (full — all 4 components)
- All four components are present: chest body, mercy seat/lid, and two carrying poles
- Components must be assembled in the correct order (poles first, then chest, then lid — or the lid can't seat correctly)
- Each component snaps into place with a distinct haptic and audio signature:
  - First pole: single clear tap
  - Second pole: two-tone tap sequence  
  - Chest body: a deeper resonant thud
  - Mercy seat/lid: a chime-like resolution note — the four notes together form a complete chord
- Wrong placement: gentle rejection animation (component slides back)
- No time limit

**The completion moment**:
All four components assembled. The Ark appears complete. The two winged figures on the lid face each other perfectly — their wingspan tips touching at the center.

**The Ark glows briefly** — a warm, complex light, visible through the Spiritual Lens (other lenses show different aspects: Technologic shows the circuit connections activating, Symbolic shows the symbol network on the surface completing, Political shows a "seal" indicator activating, Mythic shows a divine glow, Spiritual shows something indescribable — a warmth that fills the screen for 1.2 seconds then settles).

**Knowledge Key**: *"COMPLETE [INSTRUMENT]"*

**Archivist** (quietly): *"All five abilities are now accessible. I'm not sure I understand what most of them do yet. That's alright. The Ark seems to."*

## Page 7 — "Carry Constraint" (Puzzle: E2 Full)

**Format**: Navigation puzzle — Ark must be carried to the Giza approach path

**Puzzle type**: E2 Carry Constraint (full)
- The Ark (assembled) must be carried via its two carrying poles
- Two-finger drag: one finger per pole, must maintain parallel grip (hands too far apart = angle violation; too close = contact danger)
- Path from the current T5-C location to the Giza entry point (Issue 09's entrance)
- Obstacles: narrow passages (requires specific orientation — horizontal vs. vertical), Scribe corruption events that require a brief one-handed stabilize (hold one pole with one finger while stabilizing a panel with the other hand)
- 7 navigation checkpoints

**The "carry" feel**: The Ark slightly resists motion when angled incorrectly — a gentle push-back haptic. When correctly oriented and moving smoothly, a warm low pulse. The Ark has presence.

**Completion**: The Ark approaches the Giza entrance. The circuit architecture (from Issue 09's Spiritual Lens view) begins to glow as the Ark nears the structure — the structure is responding.

**Knowledge Key**: *"CARRIED"*

## Design Notes

- **The Ark Assembly chord (four component placement sounds)** is one of the most important audio design moments in the game. Each component joining should feel like adding an instrument to an orchestra — building to a complete chord that resolves. The sound design team should treat this sequence as a mini-composition.
- **D3 Identity Node resolution** in the middle of an assembly issue is deliberate — the two threads (the object and its carrier) complete at the same moment. The Ark is assembled the same issue the identity of its last carrier is confirmed.
- **Carry Constraint (E2)** is physically demanding — it requires two-finger coordination on a mobile screen. The difficulty is design-intentional. Carrying the Ark should feel like an act of responsibility, not a menu selection.
- **Fifth Ark ability (Capstone Lock) shown as locked** gives the player a persistent reminder that one action remains. It's a design hook and a narrative promise in a single UI element.
- **The Ark's response to Giza proximity** (circuit glow as Ark approaches) is the first physical confirmation that the Ark and the structure are related. The player has known this intellectually since Issue 08. Feeling it is different.
