## Issue Summary

The tone shifts. T4 is the era of erasure. The knowledge embedded in T3 is now a threat to consolidating power. The player watches — and then fights — as panels are deliberately damaged, glyphs removed, and faces chiseled away.

The Scribes (the lock faction) appear explicitly for the first time. They're not antagonists with faces — they're a process. The defacement of the historical record.

The Akhenaten/Moses identity node is the emotional pivot: a single figure at the center of two mythologies, carrying something that will not be identified in this issue. The player can't resolve the node yet — the Identity puzzle is partially completed here, and held open until Issue 10.

## Page 3 — "Defacement Undo" (Puzzle: B3)

**Format**: Full-page puzzle

**Puzzle type**: B3 Defacement Undo (first use)
- A wall of partially defaced glyphs is presented
- Below each removed/damaged glyph is a faint "ghost" — the afterimage of what was there
- Player must match the correct "ghost" glyph to its removed position
- 6 glyphs to restore (one is completely gone and must be reconstructed from context — it's the apex symbol from Issue 03's triangle)
- Political Lens: shows which glyphs were removed most recently (prioritization)
- Symbolic Lens: shows what the ghost glyphs *meant* (helps with reconstruction)
- Technologic Lens: reveals the tool marks (useful for identifying the 6th glyph by physical means)

**The 6th glyph** (the apex): Must be reconstructed from the surrounding glyph pattern — no ghost visible. Players use the Symbolic Lens to see the "shape" of the missing meaning and fill it in. If missed: puzzle completes 5/6; the 6th is locked until Issue 08.

**Completion reward**:
- Knowledge Key: *"RESTORATION"*
- Archive Notebook adds the defaced wall (before/after comparison page)
- Archivist note: *"Record T4-A — The archive was defaced from within. The remove order was issued from inside. The Scribes are not outsiders. They had keys."*

## Design Notes

- **Scribe faction as process, not villain.** The Scribes have blank-panel faces. They aren't characters with motivations you'll empathize with or resent. They are an institutional force. The Political Lens reveals them most clearly.
- **B1 Stabilize escalation**: This is the first time stabilize feels like a fight. The earlier tutorials prepared the mechanic. Issue 05 introduces the feeling that something is *working against* the player. This is the first friction.
- **The Identity Node partial completion** leaves the player with an unresolved element in their Notebook. The Archive Notebook having an open/incomplete item creates a persistent "pull" toward resolution — a design-intentional hook toward Issue 10.
- **The rectangular void** is the Ark's silhouette before the Ark is named. Every player sees it. It won't be labeled until Issue 07.
- **Tone shift from Issues 01–04 to Issues 05–08**: The earlier issues were primarily about discovery. Issue 05 introduces resistance. The Archive is now threatening to someone.
