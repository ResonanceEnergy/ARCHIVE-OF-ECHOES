## Issue Summary

The player enters the interior of the structure from Issue 06 (which is now understood to be a massive stone edifice, though still not formally named). Interior panels show narrow internal passages — the shaft system. Using a "micro-camera" interaction (dragging a small viewport around the interior of a single large-scale panel), the player navigates the labyrinth to reach the central chamber.

In the central chamber: the rectangular void. This panel, seen in glimpses since Issue 05, is now full-size and fully rendered. In the void's shadow is an object — partially visible. The Technologic Lens + Spiritual Lens together allow the player to see it clearly:

**THE ARK.** Named for the first time in the Archive Notebook.

The Ark Assembly Puzzle (E1) is shown in preview — the components are visible but scattered. One component can be recovered here. The rest will be found in Issue 10.

## Page 2 — "Shaft Labyrinth" (Puzzle: C2)

**Format**: Full-page puzzle (single large panel, interactive)

**Puzzle type**: C2 Shaft Labyrinth (full)
- The entire page is a single cross-section panel of the structure's interior — showing multiple shafts, chambers, and junctions
- A small "micro-camera" viewport (a circle, like a flashlight beam) is the player's view into the cross-section
- The player navigates the micro-camera from the entrance to the central chamber
- Path is constrained: certain shafts are too narrow (camera cannot pass), some shafts have symbolic markers indicating direction (only readable with the correct lens)
- Dead ends reveal additional content (2 hidden Knowledge Keys in dead-end chambers)
- The correct path requires alternating Technologic Lens (for shaft angle orientation) and Symbolic Lens (for junction markers)

**Important chambers** along the path:
- **Ascending shaft junction**: A branch point. Technologic Lens shows degree markings (pointing to specific stars).
- **Hidden chamber**: Reachable via a non-obvious route. Contains an Ink Restore puzzle (B2) — a cross-section diagram of the object in the central chamber, with some lines faded. Restore the lines → partial Ark preview.
- **The King's reach**: A narrow pre-chamber before the center. Archivist: *"The geometry changes here. The ceiling height triples. Whatever was placed at the center required more than a passage. It required a room."*

**Completion**: Micro-camera reaches the central chamber entrance. The puzzle page auto-zooms to the central chamber.

**Knowledge Keys**: *"PASSAGE"* (main path) + *"DESCENT"* (alternate shaft, dead-end reward)

## Page 5 — "Ink Restore" (Puzzle: B2) + Ark Preview

**Format**: Full-page puzzle

**Puzzle type**: B2 Ink Restore
- The cross-section diagram found in the hidden shaft chamber (Page 2, alternate path) is presented
- The diagram shows the Ark's construction with several lines faded or missing — the partial preview from the alternate path
- Player traces the faded lines to restore them
- 8 line segments to restore (some easy — structural outlines; some complex — the figures on the lid)
- Symbolic Lens makes the figure lines easier (reveals the "meaning" of the shapes, making the traced form feel confirming)
- Technologic Lens makes the structural outlines easier (reveals the engineering tolerance markings)

**On completion**: The full Ark diagram is restored. It now appears in the Archive Notebook as a proper technical drawing.

**Archivist note (on completion)**: *"Every measurement in this diagram corresponds to a specification. The specification was not invented here. It was received. The Architect's blueprint included this object. The grid included its place. I'm no longer archaeologizing. I'm assembling."*

**Knowledge Key**: *"BLUEPRINT"*

## Design Notes

- **The Ark naming moment (Page 4)** should feel earned. The player has seen the rectangular void in Issues 05 and 06. The Ark has been hinted. The dual-lens reveal is a ceremony — the first time two lenses must be active simultaneously, producing something neither alone can show.
- **C2 Shaft Labyrinth** is the most exploration-heavy puzzle in the game so far. The micro-camera mechanic turns a single panel into a traversable space — a key design innovation. The alternate paths reward exploration rather than punishing wrong turns.
- **Ark component #1 of 4 (the carrying pole)** is the first tangible inventory item. The Ark Inventory unlocking in the Notebook gives players a checklist — a pull toward Issues 08–11.
- **Scribe pressure escalation** on the final chamber page creates urgency where the rest of the issue was meditative. The shift from awe to urgency is deliberate pacing.
- **Issue 07 as structural climax of act 2**: After this issue, the player knows what the object is, where it belongs, and that it's incomplete. Issues 08–12 are the gathering and completion arc.
