## Issue Summary

The player enters T1 — the deepest timeline cluster. Three eras unfold:

1. **T1-A (Descent)**: The "sky beings" arrive. The player witnesses the Council Decree that established human civilization on a managed path. Puzzle: reconstruct the Decree.
2. **T1-B (E.D.I.N.)**: The founding of the first cultivation zone; Isis and the bloodline pair Adamu/Eve; the first stabilize challenge.
3. **T1-C (The Limiter)**: The hidden modification — a panel beneath a panel, showing the chromosomal constraint. First Technologic lens required here. The Djed appears for the first time as a single-bar symbol (it has 4 bars at maximum resonance; this is bar 1 of 4).

This issue is the first "lore-dense" issue. The Archive Notebook receives multiple new entries automatically.

## Page 2 — "The Council Decree" (T1-A Puzzle: D2)

**Format**: 5-panel irregular grid (Council session depicted)

**Story context**: The Sky Council convenes and issues a directive for the human experiment. The panels show a formal proclamation — but the text is fragmented. The decree has been deliberately disrupted (by whom?).

**Puzzle type**: D2 Council Decree
- Player receives 6 glyph "clauses" that must be assembled in the correct order to form the Decree
- Clauses are images+symbol sequences, not text — no reading required
- A correct assembly is one where each clause logically follows the previous (cause → effect → rule)
- Wrong assemblies result in the panels visually "arguing" with each other (ink lines don't connect)

**The Decree (assembled)**: 
*[Class I civilization established] → [Zone designated: E.D.I.N.] → [Custodian assigned: ISS/Isis] → [Subject pair: initialized] → [Duration: multi-generational] → [Review: at culmination of the cycle]*

**Completion**: Decree glows. The assembled decree fades into the Archivist's Notebook as a single "record." Knowledge Key: *"MANDATE"*

**Gutter** (between panels 3-4): *"Did anyone ask the subjects?"*

## Design Notes

- **The Limiter reveal** is this issue's thesis: the game asks the player to look at creation mythology through a Technologic lens and find something that myth left out. It is never presented as fact — it is presented as *the comic's interpretation*, which the player is exploring.
- **The Djed single bar** is a 4-part progression mechanic seeded here. The player won't know what it means yet. The Archivist notes it. The Gutter does not. That asymmetry is intentional.
- **Corruption on the E.D.I.N. page** is slightly harder than Issue 00's tutorial — this is the first time stabilize has consequence (failure = page loses content temporarily). Not punishing, but notable.
- **Issue 02 is intended for players who chose the T1 branch in Issue 01.** Players who chose T2 will enter this issue from a different narrative direction in Issue 03, and arrive at the same T1-C Limiter panel through a "flashback" page.
