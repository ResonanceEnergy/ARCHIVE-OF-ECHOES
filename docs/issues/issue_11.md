## Issue Summary

The Ark is inside Giza. The King's Chamber receives it. The coffer's circuit architecture (first seen in Issue 09's Spiritual Lens view) activates — partially. The circuit runs through the entire structure but requires the Djed Pillar as an intermediary stabilizer. The player finds the Djed position (encoded in T3-A's blueprint, now readable with full Constellation Map context) and activates it in a 4-bar sequence — each bar corresponding to a timeline cluster's resolved panel.

Then: E3 Circuit Completion. The boss puzzle. A complex, multi-lens puzzle that requires the player to trace the circuit's path through the entire structure, identify break points (Scribe interference), and repair them using all five Ark abilities and all five lenses in combination.

The Scribes make their final move — a total disruption attempt. The Resonants appear fully for the first time — not as margin text, but as in-panel figures who fight the Scribe corruption alongside the player.

4 Djed bars active = 4 completed. The 5th and final element — the capstone — is the only thing left. Issue 12.

## Page 4 — "Circuit Completion" (Boss Puzzle: E3)

**Format**: Full-page boss puzzle (2 pages mechanically — pages 4–5)

**Puzzle type**: E3 Circuit Completion (full)

**Scale**: The complete cross-section of Giza is now the puzzle board. The circuit path runs from:
`Ark (coffer) → Djed Pillar → Chamber walls → Shafts → Surface → Capstone slot`

**The puzzle**:
- The full circuit path is shown in its Spiritual Lens visualization
- 9 break points (Scribe interference — they've reached inside the structure and disrupted 9 nodes)
- Each break point requires a different repair action:
  - B1 Stabilize (3 nodes)
  - B3 Defacement Undo (2 nodes — glyphs removed from circuit alignment marks)
  - C1 Alignment (1 node — a shaft angle has been physically disrupted; must re-align)
  - Ark ability: Seal Burn (1 node — a Scribe lock on a circuit junction)
  - Ark ability: Scan (1 node — a hidden bypass is needed; Scan reveals it)
  - Ark ability: Circuit Link (1 node — two separated circuit nodes need manual reconnection)

**All five lenses used**:
- Mythic: reveals the spiritual significance of each break point
- Technologic: shows the structural mechanics of the break
- Symbolic: reveals what each break point's glyph means (context for repair)
- Political: shows the Scribe intervention marks at each node (distinguishes Scribe breaks from natural decay)
- Spiritual: shows the circuit flow itself (required to track whether each repair actually worked)

**Progress indicator**: The circuit diagram fills in as each break is repaired. 9 nodes → each repair illuminates one section.

**Completion (from page 5)**: The last node repaired. The circuit activates fully — from coffer to Djed to all chambers to all shafts to the surface. The entire cross-section glows in Spiritual Lens, every line lit.

Except: the apex. The capstone position. Dark. Waiting.

## Design Notes

- **E3 Circuit Completion (9-node boss puzzle)** is the most mechanically demanding puzzle in the game. Using all five lenses and all four active Ark abilities is the design intent — the game gives the player a full instrument and asks them to play it. The puzzle shouldn't feel hard; it should feel like a capstone exercise in mastery.
- **Scribes' final move → Resonants hold the line** is the narrative payoff for the Scribe escalation arc. The Scribes lose. They are overcome — not by the player, but by the collective context of all the readers who stayed. The player's role was to get far enough that the Resonants could finish it.
- **Djed 4-bar activation using all four prior mechanics** (stabilize, alignment, mirror, defacement) is the game's clearest "call-back pattern" — the player has been practicing for this activation since Issue 02.
- **The Resonants leader with Architect proportions** is intentional ambiguity. The Architect never left the knowledge network. Whether the Resonant leader IS the Architect, is descended from the Architect's doctrine, or is simply an inheritor of the pattern — the game does not confirm. Different lenses suggest different readings.
- **Issue 11 as the penultimate issue** should feel like the held breath before the exhale. Everything is in place. The player goes into Issue 12 knowing exactly what must be done and exactly what it will mean.
