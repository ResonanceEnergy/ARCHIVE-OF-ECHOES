## Issue Summary

T3 is the knowledge layer — the era when what was *done* in T1 and T2 was encoded into patterns, symbols, and architectural correspondences so that future generations could recover it. The Architect (the figure associated with Thoth, Hermes, Metatron — never definitively named) appears for the first time. The player must navigate two knowledge sites:

1. **T3-A (Thoth's Blueprint)**: The Architect is encoding the principles of the cosmos into geometric structures. The C3 Correspondence puzzle requires the player to match celestial positions with ground structures — "As Above, So Below."
2. **T3-B (Mystery Schools)**: The Gutter teaches itself. Hidden teachings encoded in architecture. The Glyph Evolution chains become fully active. A Mirror-City puzzle intro reveals T3 as a "copy" of something in the sky.

By the end of Issue 04, all five lenses are unlocked. The player now has the full instrument.

## Page 2 — "As Above, So Below" (Puzzle: C3 Correspondence)

**Format**: Split-field puzzle page

**Puzzle type**: C3 Correspondence
- Top half of screen: star chart (sky, specific constellation positions)
- Bottom half: empty ground grid
- Player must drag architectural markers onto the ground grid to match the star positions above
- 7 markers to place
- Scale and proportion matter (markers are different sizes; they must be placed in correct relative positions)
- Feedback: lines appear between each marker and its corresponding star as they are placed correctly

**Correct configuration**: The constellation layout, when placed on the ground, produces — without being explicitly indicated — a triangle shape pointing north. The outline of three major buildings connected would form this shape.

**Complexity**: The 7th marker (the smallest) goes in the center-triangle apex. It's easy to miss. Without it, the puzzle resolves but shows a faint "incomplete" indicator on the Notebook.

**Completion reward**:
- Knowledge Key: *"CORRESPONDENCE"* (deepened from Issue 03's version of the same word)
- The triangle (▲) in the Archive Notebook gains a second tier: `▲ + a dot at apex = incomplete structure`
- Archivist note: *"The alignment isn't decorative. It's load-bearing. The architecture participates in something larger than architecture."*

**Gutter** (during puzzle): *"The apex is the last thing you place. The apex is always the last thing."*

- 5 glyph stages presented in scrambled order
- Each stage shows the glyph evolving: its original form → simplified → adopted into a different culture → modified → final form recognizable in later history
- Player must arrange them in evolutionary sequence
- Feedback: "evolutionary arrows" connect correct sequential pairs

**The glyph chain**: An ancient symbol → progressively simplified and absorbed into Egyptian hieroglyphs → borrowed into Semitic proto-alphabet → arrives at a modern letter (not specified — implied by shape)

**Correct order**: [Original complex form] → [Kemet simplified] → [Sumer variant] → [Phoenician abstraction] → [Final recognizable shape]

**Complexity modifier**: Two middle stages can be confused. The correct order is determined by the internal structure (the "memory" of the previous form is still visible inside the simplified version — the Symbolic Lens makes this easier to see).

**Completion reward**:

## Design Notes

- **Issue 04 is the pivot from "discovery" to "interpretation."** With all five lenses active, the player is now equipped to read the same content five different ways. This changes how they'll approach Issues 05–08.
- **The Architect never speaks.** Everything the player knows about this figure comes from: what they built, what they left, and what the Gutter says about them. No dialogue. No name. This is design-intentional — the Architect is a function, not a character with a story arc.
- **Glyph Evolution full unlock** in this issue establishes the mechanic that will be critical in Issue 05 (Defacement — when glyphs are *removed* rather than evolved).
- **The Gutter's escalating intelligence**: By Issue 04, the Gutter is teaching things the Archivist hasn't figured out yet. This is deliberate. The two entities have different relationships to the Archive. The Gutter is the Archive's subconscious.
- **All Five Lenses active = the player is now "fully instrumented."** Issues 05–08 will use lens combinations and lens conflicts in ways that couldn't be done with fewer than five lenses.
