## Issue Summary

The player explores T2 — the era of the first organized cities. Three geographical nodes:

1. **T2-A (Sumer)**: The first city. Depicted as an intentional design — not organic growth. A pan-panel reorder reveals the migration that led to it.
2. **T2-B (Kemet)**: Egypt after the flood-rebuild. The geometry of settlement matches Sumer exactly — impossible coincidence. First use of Overlay puzzle comparing two timeszones' plans.
3. **T2-C (Atlantis)**: Optional branch. A concentric-ring city plan appears in the gutter. Following it leads to an optional shard (no mandatory puzzle, but adds a Constellation Map node and a Knowledge Key).

**The issue's hidden pivot**: The cities are too similar. The Symbolic Lens reveals the overlap. A single symbol — a triangle with an open apex — appears in all three locations. The player doesn't yet know it's a pyramid.

## Page 2 — "Migration Reorder" (T2-A Puzzle: A1)

**Format**: Full-page puzzle — 6 panels showing the stages of city founding, scrambled

**Puzzle (A1 Panel Reorder — moderate)**:
Scrambled panels show:
- An empty plain
- Survey figures moving across the plain (with measuring tools)
- Stakes placed in specific positions
- Foundation stones laid
- First structures complete
- The city from above — perfectly aligned to cardinal directions

**Correct order**: Plain → Survey → Stakes → Foundation → Structures → City-from-above

**Complexity modifier**: Two panels are "interchangeable" at first look (Survey and Stakes could be read in either order). The correct solution (Stakes before Survey) is indicated by the direction of the surveyors' gaze — they look toward the stakes, not away from them. No hint given — players must look at the art.

**Completion reward**:
- Knowledge Key: *"GRID"*
- A faint triangle symbol appears in the city-from-above panel's center. Without the Symbolic Lens it's invisible. With it: a glowing triangle. (Symbolic Lens not yet unlocked — this is a seed for later.)

## Page 4 — "Overlay" (Puzzle: A4)

**Format**: Overlay puzzle interface

**Puzzle type**: A4 Overlay (first use)
- Player drags the Sumer page over the Kemet page
- When aligned correctly (by matching the orientation marks — stars visible in both panels' sky), the two grids overlap perfectly
- A third form appears in the overlap — a triangle with an open apex, at the exact center of both cities
- The triangle glows briefly, then fades

**On completion**:
- Knowledge Key: *"CORRESPONDENCE"*
- Archive Notebook auto-draws the two city plans over each other — the triangle is preserved there
- Archivist note: *"The triangle at center. Both cities. Same position. Same proportions. I have no explanation for this. I'm going to keep that in mind."*

**Gutter text** (appears during the overlay): *"Pattern recognition is how you know you're awake."*

## Design Notes

- **Issue 03 is the "Technologic → Symbolic" pivot issue.** The player entered T2 with an engineering mindset (from the Limiter reveal in Issue 02, or fresh from T1 branch logic); by end they're starting to see symbols as a *layer on top* of the engineering.
- **The triangle (▲) is the pyramid in disguise.** Players may or may not realize this in Issue 03. The Archivist never says "pyramid." The Gutter says "▲." The reveal happens in Issue 09 when the completed Giza panel is seen.
- **Overlay mechanic (A4)** is introduced as a gentle two-page puzzle; it scales dramatically in later issues (Issue 08 uses 5-page overlay for the convergence panel).
- **G2 Gutter Whisper Trace** is visually subtle. First-time players who don't find it should still complete the issue — the traced symbol just appears unlabeled. The Notebook has it either way; the trace just "unlocks" the name the Gutter gives it.
- **Atlantis Deep** is flagged as optional and fragmentary — maintaining the fantasy-label discipline. The Archive doesn't claim Atlantis was real. The comic *depicts a city in a myth* and asks the player to find what pattern it shares with documented cities.
