## Issue Summary

Issue 08 is the pivot between the game's second act (discovery) and third act (completion). All five timeline clusters are now active. The player assembles the 5-page Overlay — the largest and most complex puzzle in the game — connecting all of the threads they've followed. When the 5 pages align, the Constellation Map inside the Archive Notebook spontaneously rearranges: the lit nodes form a pyramid silhouette. The word "GIZA" appears for the first time in the Archivist's notes.

T5 (The Missing Center) unlocks. The locked thread — the one glimpsed faintly since Issue 06 — is now accessible. The player is shown its entry point but cannot enter it until Issue 09.

The Scribes mount their most intense intervention yet. The Resonants (the reader faction) appear for the first time, briefly — as an act of rescue.

## Page 2 — "Semantic Merge" (Puzzle: D4)

**Format**: Full-page puzzle

**Puzzle type**: D4 Semantic Merge/Split
- 5 symbolic terms are presented: each has been "split" across timelines (different cultures named the same concept with different symbols)
- Player must merge each pair (or trio) of symbols into a single unified form — dragging overlapping symbols together until they fuse into one
- The 5 terms: [STABILITY], [CIRCUIT], [CARRY], [SEAL], [COMPLETE]
- Each merged symbol becomes a component of the final panel showing the Ark's five functional abilities (the Ark system from GDD)
- If already familiar with the Ark from Issue 07, players recognize these as the Ark's functions

**Complexity**: One pair ([CIRCUIT] and [SEAL]) look visually similar when presented separately — they can be incorrectly merged. The wrong merge produces an "unstable" fused form (wobbles, looks wrong). Symbolic Lens distinguishes them clearly.

**Completion reward**:
- Knowledge Key: *"THE FIVE"*
- Archive Notebook: The Ark's Notebook entry gains an abilities section with all 5 listed
- Archivist note: *"The Ark isn't just a container. It's an instrument. These are its notes."*

## Page 3 — "The Five-Page Overlay" (Puzzle: A4 5-page variant)

**Format**: Extended puzzle sequence (takes up pages 3–4, mechanically)

**Puzzle type**: A4 Overlay (5-page — grand variant)
- All five timeline pages are now available:
  - Page A (T1 Origins: descent, E.D.I.N., Limiter)
  - Page B (T2 Cities: Sumer, Kemet, alignment)
  - Page C (T3 Architect: blueprint, mirror-city)
  - Page D (T4 Break: defacement, Exile, vault)
  - Page E (T5 — partially blank; the "missing center")
- Pages must be stacked with Page A at bottom, building up chronologically
- Each page has a reference symbol (the triangle ▲) that serves as the alignment anchor — all five must be centered on ▲
- Page E (T5 — partially blank) acts as a semi-transparent overlay: where it has content, it fills in gaps in the lower pages; where it's blank, the lower pages show through

**The reveal**: When all five are aligned and stacked, the composite forms a single coherent image:
- At the bottom: stars and sky beings (T1)
- In the middle: cities and grid (T2)
- Above: the blueprint (T3)
- Overlaid: the exile path (T4)
- At the top center: the blank of T5, with one element visible — the capstone position

The shape is unmistakable: a cross-section of Giza. All five eras, stacked, forming the complete structure.

**Music**: Full Mythic motif activates, using all five lens root notes in sequence on completion. The most musically complex moment in the game.

## Design Notes

- **Issue 08 is the "everything connects" issue — the emotional and structural peak of Act 2.** It should feel like a reward for all the work done in Issues 01–07. The detective board pyramid reveal is designed to feel earned rather than given.
- **"GIZA" first appearing in the Archivist's own handwriting** (not in a text prompt, not in a caption box) is a deliberate presentation choice. The player sees the Archivist realizing it — not being told it.
- **5-page Overlay** is longer than any previous puzzle. It should be paced as a ritual, not a challenge — the pages align easily if the player has been attentive. The music is its own reward.
- **Resonants introduction** is brief and mysterious. They save the Archive from the Scribe attack but don't explain themselves. They write "Not today" in the margin and nothing else. Players learn more about them in Issue 11.
- **T5 unlock via Knowledge Key combination** rewards players who have been attentive to the Key system since Issue 01. The 5 keys required are all from milestone puzzles — no grinding, no obscure puzzle.
- **The four-panel "what comes next" page (Page 7)** is the closest the game comes to a "previously on" style preview. It's intentionally impressionistic — not a checklist, but a feeling. The last panel (Issue 12's blank panel) tells the player what but not how.
