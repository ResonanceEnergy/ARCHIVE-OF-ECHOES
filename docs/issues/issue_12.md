## Issue Summary

Issue 12 is the last chapter. There are no new mechanics to learn, no new factions, no new timeline clusters. Everything is already in place. The issue is about delivery.

The capstone placement is the final puzzle. It occurs in two phases:
1. **Approach**: Carrying the capstone (which the player finds in the T5-E apex chamber) to the slot
2. **Placement**: E4 Capstone Placement — a precision placement using the E4 mechanic previewed in Issue 09, now executed with the Ark's Capstone Lock ability

The active lens at the moment of placement determines which ending variant the player receives. The choice is implicit — players don't select an ending from a menu. They select a lens (as they have done throughout the game) and place the capstone. The lens determines the meaning of what just happened.

After placement: the circuit closes. The pyramid completes. The Ark's light runs to the apex and seals. **The player ejects.** The 2100 world returns.

The epilogue is three panels.

## Page 4 — "E4 Capstone Placement" (Final Puzzle)

**Format**: Full-page puzzle — final

**Puzzle type**: E4 Capstone Placement (full, with Capstone Lock)

**Setup**:
- The capstone slot is shown from directly above — a triangular opening, precisely cut
- The capstone is shown from above in the player's hands
- The capstone must be oriented to match the slot (4 possible orientations — 3 wrong, 1 correct)
- The correct orientation is indicated by subtle marks on the capstone's edges (visible with Technologic Lens) and by the Djed circuit lines pointing toward the correct edge (visible with Spiritual Lens)
- Once orientated correctly: the **Capstone Lock ability** activates — the Ark's light (running through the circuit from the coffer far below) rises through the shaft and meets the capstone's underside, illuminating it with a warm lock confirmation

**The Placement**:
Player holds the correctly-oriented capstone over the slot. With both thumbs, the player lowers it (two-finger vertical drag downward, maintaining parallel grip — mirroring the Carry Constraint mechanic from Issue 10, but inverted).

**The capstone descends.**

**The slot receives it.**

**The click.**

## Design Notes

- **The lens-dependent ending** is the most important design decision in Issue 12. Players choose an ending by choosing a lens — which is how they've been making meaning throughout the entire game. The ending is the culmination of that identity. No menu, no dialogue tree, no explicit ending selector. The lens *is* the choice.
- **The three ending variants are all valid and complete.** No ending is worse than another. Locked Stability is not a bad ending because it doesn't "propagate" — it is complete. The Gutter Path is not a secret ending — it is available to careful players who engaged with the Gutter throughout.
- **The final caption** ("Completion does not mean certainty. It means the story can continue.") applies equally to all three endings, to the player's experience of the game, and to the fictional universe. It is the closest thing to a thesis statement the game has.
- **Demolition equipment parked** in the 2100 epilogue: the player did not stop the demolition through any in-game action. The demolition is presumably postponed by the classification change — but the game does not confirm this. The story can continue. In a future season or never. The Archive survives.
- **"The Gutter in the next one" (Gutter entity's final line)**: season 2 tease. Not required to be built. Honest to the character.
- **Issue 12's overall tone**: The longest pause in any game the player has ever played. Ceremony. Silence. The payoff for every puzzle solved, every panel restored, every glyph recovered. It should feel sacred without being solemn. Complete without being closed.
