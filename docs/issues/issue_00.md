## Issue Summary

The player arrives in the world of 2100 as a nameless Archivist-trainee assigned to a condemned section of the physical Archive — a vast material library of pre-digital media maintained as cultural heritage. The sector is scheduled for demolition. One analog comic, unlisted in any catalog, is found in a corrupted storage slot.

The player examines it. It pulls them in.

## Page 1 — "The Assignment"

**Format**: 5-panel grid (2 + 3)

| Panel | Type | Content |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1 | Static | Exterior. The Archive building (brutalist concrete, 2100 city looming behind). Small figure (player-character) entering. Caption: *"Sector 7-D. Demolition scheduled: 72 hours."* |
| 2 | Static | Interior. Long rows of analog media. Dust. Yellow caution light. Caption: *"Personal assignment: catalog and clear."* |
| 3 | Static | Close-up of hands skimming shelves. All items labeled. All accounted for. |
| 4 | Static | One slot. No label. Just a faint shimmer. Caption: *"Almost all."* |
| 5 | Interact (TUTORIAL) | The unlabeled slot. Glow pulses faintly. **Tutorial prompt appears**: *"Tap to inspect."* |

**Gameplay**: Tap panel 5 to advance. No puzzle. Pure tutorial.

**Audio**: Ambient hum of preservation systems. Faint creak of old shelving. On panel 5: a low harmonic tone (panel is "alive").

**Archivist Note** (margin): *"Unlisted objects become property of the Archive-at-large. Standard procedure: log and contain."*

## Page 2 — "The Object"

**Format**: 3-panel strip (full-width panels, stacked)

| Panel | Type | Content |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1 | Static | The comic book. Full color — **the first full-color image in the game**. Title barely visible: "ARCHIVE OF ECHOES." The 2100 world around it is desaturated. |
| 2 | Interact (STABILIZE tutorial) | Panel is "corrupted" — slightly warped. **Tutorial prompt**: *"Something is wrong with this panel. Long-press to stabilize."* Player long-presses the panel; it "settles" — the distortion calms. |
| 3 | Static | The comic is still. Solid. Looking back at the player. A pair of ancient eyes looks out from the cover illustration. Caption from the Gutter (between panels): *"Contained? Sure. If that helps you sleep."* |

**Gameplay**: First stabilize tutorial. No failure state. Press and hold correctly → immediate success.

**Gutter text** (between panels 2–3): *"Rules are for things that stay where you put them."*

**Archivist Note**: *"Stabilization complete. Object logged. Timestamp: 2100.11.04.17:23. Anomaly: item resists standard cataloging format. Classification: PENDING."*

## Page 3 — "The Pull"

**Format**: 2 large panels + 1 full-bleed splash panel

| Panel | Type | Content |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1 | Static | The player-character opens the comic to page one. The first panel inside glows. Not from any light source — from within. |
| 2 | Interact (PINCH-TO-ENTER tutorial) | Close-up of the open comic. The first interior panel pulses. **Tutorial prompt**: *"This panel wants you inside. Pinch to enter."* |
| SPLASH | Full-bleed | **THE PULL**: The player-character is depicted tumbling through a visual vortex of panels — fragments of all twelve issues swirl past, impressionistic, incomplete, impossible to read yet. The frame of the comic expands to fill the world. The 2100 world shrinks to a postage-stamp in the corner and winks out. Caption: *"You were supposed to clear this sector."* Gutter text (full page): *"You were supposed to leave."* |

**Gameplay**: Pinch tutorial on panel 2. Initiates non-interactive splash cinematic (auto-advance after 3 seconds or tap to skip).

**Sound**: On pinch: dramatic bass drop. During splash: rising orchestral swell (Mythic motif, not resolved). Cuts to silence for the first panel of Issue 01.

**Haptic**: On pinch: heavy thump. During splash cinematic: slow rising pulse, then full stop.

## Design Notes

- **No fail states in Issue 00.** This is pure onboarding.
- **Color language established**: 2100 world = desaturated. Comic interior = full color. This rule holds forever.
- **The Gutter entity speaks for the first time** in panel 2, page 2 — but is not named, explained, or labeled. Players who find it early feel rewarded. Players who miss it encounter The Gutter later in Issue 01 as a surprise.
- **The Pull splash** shows brief fragments of future issues. Nothing is readable, but art-conscious players can screenshot and zoom. Easter egg for the obsessive.
