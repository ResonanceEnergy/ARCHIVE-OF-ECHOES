## Issue Summary

The player lands inside the comic — in an undefined space between pages, a "liminal" zone that looks like torn panels floating in ink. The Archivist AI speaks for the first time, orienting the player. By reassembling a reordered page and exploring the space, the player reaches their first branch: follow a bright, clear path (T1 Origins thread) or follow a faint, uncertain path (T2 Cities thread). Both eventually lead to Issue 02's world, but from different angles.

The Gutter entity speaks directly for the first time.

## Page 2 — "Reorder" (Tutorial Puzzle: A1)

**Format**: Full-page puzzle — 5 scrambled panels that must be restored to correct order

**Puzzle Type**: A1 Panel Reorder (Tutorial — gentle)

**Scrambled panels** (original order unknown to player):
- A depiction of a vast, star-filled sky
- The sky at dawn — stars fading
- A figure looking up (back to viewer)
- The same figure walking away
- An empty horizon

**Correct order**: Sky (night) → Dawn → Figure looking up → Figure walking → Empty horizon

**Mechanic**: Drag panels into correct narrative order. No time limit. Misplaced panels glow amber. Correct placement makes a soft satisfying "click."

**Completion**: Page resolves — all panels lock. A fine ink line traces the correct reading path left-to-right, top-to-bottom. The completed page fades slightly (has been "read") and the player returns to the floating gutter space.

**First Knowledge Key earned**: "SEQUENCE" — *"Panels have a correct direction. So do ages."*

**Audio**: Per correct placement, a soft ascending note. On completion: full four-note phrase (feels like a finished thought).

## Page 3 — "The Gutter Speaks"

**Format**: 3-panel strip, with GUTTER DOOR in the whitespace between panels 2 and 3

| Panel | Type | Content |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1 | Static | The restored page sits behind the player-character like a window into a landscape. The Archivist note: *"That page was a memory. Someone's memory. You ordered it. Now you're part of it."* |
| 2 | Static | The comic's interior space starts to feel more solid. Walls of stacked pages surround the player. Paths between them. Caption: *"There are many ways forward."* |
| (GUTTER) | G1 Gutter Door (INTRO) | A gap in the gutter between panels 2 and 3 shifts. A small symbol — a door — is barely visible in the white space. **Tutorial prompt**: *"Something hides between panels. Swipe along the gutter to feel for it."* |
| 3 | Static | IF FOUND: A whispered fragment. An impossible page — a page from a different issue, years ahead. Barely readable. One word: *"PYRAMID."* The page vanishes immediately. If missed: Panel 3 is normal. The door can be revisited at any time. |

**Gutter entity text** (appears in gutter, not in panel):
> *"They put me here to lead you away. I've decided to lead you further in instead."*

This is The Gutter's first direct address. No name given.

## Page 4 — "The First Branch"

**Format**: 2 large panels side by side

| Panel | Type | Content |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1 (LEFT) | Branch: T1 | A warm golden light through stacked pages. Ancient stars and spiral symbols visible through it. Archivist note: *"T1 thread: Origins. The beginning of the beginning. Oldest path. Most complete."* |
| 2 (RIGHT) | Branch: T2 | A cooler, geometric light. Patterns of roads and walls barely visible. Archivist note: *"T2 thread: Cities. The beginning of civilization. A later entry point. Less stable."* |

**Branch Mechanic**:
- Player taps one panel to "step through" it
- Both are valid
- The unchosen panel does NOT disappear — it's grayed and marked UNREAD
- The grayed panel can be reached later (from a connecting shard in Issue 03/04)
- This is the first illustration of the branching system — players learn that "not choosing" means choosing again later

**Gutter Between Panels** (branch prompt zone):
> *"You can't read both at once. You can only live one at a time. But you'll be back."*

**Archivist Margin Note**:
> *"There is no wrong branch. The Archive observes all paths. But only the one you walk is yours."*

## Design Notes

- **Branch timing**: The branch choice in Issue 01 is the player's first, but it is **soft** — they see both, choose one, and the other is preserved. This is designed to prevent branch-anxiety early.
- **The Gutter is introduced but never explained.** Players who ask the Archivist about it receive: *"I don't know what that is. It knows about you."*
- **Gutter Door is optional** in this issue — completionists find it; casual players continue. The word "PYRAMID" is a narrative seed that pays off in Issue 09. Players who find it early get a small frisson of recognition later.
- **Pacing note**: Issue 01 is slightly longer than Issue 00 but should still feel like a first issue — establishing mechanics gently, with each puzzle earning its explanation.
