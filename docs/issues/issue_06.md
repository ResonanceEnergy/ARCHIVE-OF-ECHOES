## Issue Summary

Issue 06 is the geometery issue. The player synthesizes what they know about the three city-clusters (Sumer, Kemet, and the Architect's mirror sites) into a single correspondence map. The key puzzle: the ground grid is rotated 15.5° relative to true north — and that rotation matches a specific astronomical position. When corrected, the three city nodes align into a recognizable pattern.

The pyramid silhouette appears for the first time — not named, not labeled, but undeniable. A flash of the larger structure visible at the center of the aligned map. Then gone. The Archive Notebook's detective board now has two strong connections (triangle + alignment) pulling toward the same shape.

## Page 2 — "Cardinal Alignment" (Puzzle: C1 Full)

**Format**: Full-page puzzle

**Puzzle type**: C1 Cardinal Alignment (advanced)
- Ground map (the Sumer-Kemet-Atlantis overlay from Issue 03) is presented
- Star map showing the target epoch sky is shown in upper corner (reference only)
- Player uses a rotation dial to adjust the ground map to the 15.5° target
- The target is not given numerically — the player must rotate until the ground map's key markers align with the corresponding stars
- Feedback: star-to-ground lines appear as the angle approaches correct (all 7 correspondence lines from Issue 04's C3 puzzle become visible simultaneously)
- At exactly correct angle: all 7 lines lock, the overlay glows, and the map settles

**The revelation**: When aligned, the 3 city points (Sumer / Kemet / Atlantis) form a triangle on the ground. The triangle's overall shape — proportions, orientation — is identical to the Giza Plateau layout (which the player does not yet know is what they're mapping).

**Completion reward**:
- Knowledge Key: *"ALIGNMENT"*
- The Archive Notebook adds the aligned composite map
- Archivist note: *"Three sites. One sky. One angle. One intention. I don't know what to call this yet. I'm calling it: the project."*

## Page 3 — "Mirror-City" (Puzzle: C4 Full)

**Format**: Split-screen puzzle (sky vs. ground)

**Puzzle type**: C4 Mirror-City (full)
- Top half: the sky-city (the Architect's celestial grid from Issue 04)
- Bottom half: the ground-city (the composite T2/T3 map, now aligned)
- 5 corresponding sites must be matched across the two halves
- Each pair is linked by a dotted line that only becomes visible when both pieces are correctly placed
- The Symbolic Lens shows the meaning of each correspondence (element/principle)
- The Technologic Lens shows the physical measurements (the proportional relationship)

**Completion**: All 5 pairs linked. The composite diagram is now a complete "mirror" — above and below locked in correspondence.

**The flash**: On completion, the complete mirror composite pulses once — and for 1.5 seconds, the shape of the complete structure at the center of the alignment is visible in outline: a massive triangular form with a flat base and four angled sides. It's unmistakable. Then the image settles back into diagram form.

**No caption. No Archivist note on this panel. The Gutter says:**
> *"You see it."*

## Page 4 — "The Three-Page Overlay" (Puzzle: A4 Extended)

**Format**: 3-page layered overlay puzzle (longest puzzle in the game so far)

**Puzzle type**: A4 Overlay (3-page variant)
- Player has three "pages" to stack:
  - Page A: Issue 02's T1 cluster (Origins: sky-being grid, E.D.I.N. plan)
  - Page B: Issue 03's T2 cluster (Cities: Sumer/Kemet/Atlantis composite)
  - Page C: Issue 04's T3 cluster (Architect's blueprint)
- Pages must be stacked in the correct order (A bottom, C top) and aligned using their shared reference markers (the triangle symbol ▲ appears on all three at the same position)
- When all three are correctly overlaid and aligned: the structure visible in Page 3's flash is now permanent in the overlay — its outline shows across all three eras

**Completion reward**:
- Knowledge Key: *"LAYERS"*
- Archive Notebook: the three-page overlay is stored as a single image (the pyramid outline superimposed on three timelines)
- Archivist note: *"Three eras. One object at the center. The T1 blueprint includes it. The T2 cities orbit it. The T3 architect encoded it. It was there the whole time. I was reading layers without seeing the shape they made."*

**Gutter text** (during overlay, when any 2 of 3 are aligned): *"Two mirrors make a corridor. Three make a room."*

## Design Notes

- **Issue 06 is the "magnitude shift" issue.** Issues 01–05 were about finding information. Issue 06 is when the information assembles itself into a shape that is larger than any single piece. The player should feel the weight of what they've been building toward.
- **The pyramid is never explicitly named in Issue 06.** The Archivist says "the shape." The Gutter says "▲." The pyramid is named by the player's recognition — and confirmed in Issue 09. Any earlier explicit naming would reduce the experiential impact.
- **Scribe escalation (page 5)** transitions the Archive from a discovery space to a contested space. From Issue 06 onward, every unresolved/corrupted panel across all issues gains a faint Scribe overlay — a subtle visual pressure that rewards completion.
- **Multi-lens requirement (Symbolic + Technologic)** in Issue 06 is the first time the game explicitly asks the player to switch lenses within a single page to see different content. The pyramid silhouette is only visible with Symbolic Lens — this teaches the player that the Symbolic Lens is the "shape-revealing" tool.
- **3-page Overlay** is the longest puzzle so far. It should feel like a ceremony, not just a mechanic. The pace of the puzzle is slow and deliberate — the music shifts to the full Mythic motif as all three pages align.
