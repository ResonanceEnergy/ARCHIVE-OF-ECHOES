# TIMELINE — ARCHIVE OF ECHOES: THE ANUNNAKI LEGACY

> **Fantasy Label**: All timeframes are fictional narrative spaces, not historical claims.

---

## How to Read This Document

Two parallel timelines run simultaneously:

**FRAME TIMELINE (2100)** — The "real" present of the game. Reacts to everything the player discovers.

**SHARD TIMELINE (Mythic Past)** — The narrative spaces inside the comic. Non-linear. All eras coexist.

The player moves between both. Frame timeline events are triggered by Shard discoveries.

---

## FRAME TIMELINE — Year 2100

### F-01 | PROLOGUE: The Archive
- **Event**: Protagonist arrives at a forgotten sector of the Archive of Echoes
- **State**: World is hollow, functional, meaningless
- **Discovery**: An unindexed physical comic book
- **Trigger**: Protagonist touches the comic → pulled inside
- **Characters present**: Protagonist, Archivist (detected as absence)

### F-02 | FACTION EMERGENCE (triggered after Issue 03)
- **Event**: The Scribes and Resonants become aware the protagonist is inside a shard
- **Scribes**: Try to lock/censor panels from outside
- **Resonants**: Try to amplify signals and expand branches
- **Effect on gameplay**: Defacement events begin (Scribe interference); hidden panels become occasionally visible (Resonant assistance)

### F-03 | ARCHIVIST CONTACT (triggered after Issue 06)
- **Event**: The Archivist's voice becomes clear for the first time
- **Message**: *"Multiple convergence points detected. This shard is not catalogued. Proceed with caution."*
- **Effect**: Timeline Constellation Map updates to show partial pyramid silhouette

### F-04 | CONVERGENCE ALERT (triggered after Issue 08)
- **Event**: Archive systems flag narrative instability increasing
- **Archivist**: *"Something was removed to prevent completion. It remains present in altered form."*
- **Effect**: T5 (The Missing Center) timeline unlocks

### F-05 | ARK IDENTIFICATION (triggered after Issue 09)
- **Event**: Archive cross-references all shard data; a consistent artifact signature emerges
- **Archivist**: Names the Ark formally for the first time
- **Effect**: Ark assembly gameplay begins in Issue 10

### F-06 | FINALE: Return
- **Event**: Final panel completed → Protagonist ejects from the comic
- **World state**: Meaning returns. Coherence upgrade. Utopia (not loud — quiet and warm)
- **Final action**: Protagonist closes the comic, places it back

## SHARD TIMELINE — The Mythic Past

All timeframes below exist simultaneously inside the comic — they are visited in Issue order but are not chronologically obligatory.

---

### ERA T1-A | THE DESCENT
**Fantasy time marker**: Deep past (≈450,000 years — in-universe label only)
**Theme**: Arrival. Authority. The first claim.

**Events (fantasy)**:
- Sky beings (Anunnaki archetype) descend
- "Those who came from heaven to earth" — identity myth established
- Bloodline authority doctrine begins: "right to rule" encoded in the first panels
- The Council of Fate convenes: a governing body of non-human figures who decree civilizational rules

**Key panels**:
- Sky splits open; silhouetted figures descend (ambiguous: divine? technological? symbolic?)
- First human tribes observe from below
- "Crown glyph" forms — then dissolves into a spiral (chromosome echo — planted early)

**Gameplay in this era**:
- Panel stabilization (corrupted timestamps distort the era marker)
- "Crown → DNA" logic puzzle (Lens: Mythic = crown as divine / Technologic = crown as genetic marker)
- First branch: follow Scribes into ordered panels OR follow Resonants into the ambiguous gutter route

---

### ERA T1-B | E.D.I.N.
**Fantasy time marker**: Origin era (≈200,000 years — in-universe label only)
**Theme**: Creation. Design. First constraint.

**Events (fantasy)**:
- The laboratory space called E.D.I.N. — not a garden; a ritual-science hybrid chamber
- Isis archetype proposes creation method: egg + "essence" of the sky being
- Gestation and birth of Adamu (first man archetype)
- Creation of Eve archetype — "iteration succeeds"
- E.D.I.N. becomes a "mating matrix" for population seeding

**Key panels**:
- Crystal-glass chamber with biological vats
- Isis holding the newborn: *"My hands have created it."*
- Adamu's eyes reflect a barcode (created artifact motif)
- "Bingo it worked" stamp appears as a panel border decoration

**Gameplay in this era**:
- Sequence puzzle: correct order of "creation steps" (wrong order = corrupted panel)
- Panel restore: Eve panel begins incomplete — player traces missing linework
- Choice: "Born" vs "Built" — affects Resonance Traits (Wonder +1 vs Control +1)

---

### ERA T1-C | THE LIMITER
**Fantasy time marker**: Same era as E.D.I.N.
**Theme**: Imposed mortality. Control through limitation.

**Events (fantasy)**:
- Chromosome structure is altered (fantasy: "the fusing of the seam")
- Telomere caps applied — lifespan shortened
- The change is framed as deliberate and functional: "a short life is easier to govern"
- Modern science gloss used as flavor only (not claimed as fact)

**Key panels**:
- Two chromosome panels — one intact, one mid-fusion
- A timer appears in a panel for the first time (foreshadows time mechanics)
- The Gutter whispers: *"A short life is easier to govern."*

**Gameplay**:
- "Fuse" puzzle: join two panels at their borders — creates a new insight
- Moral choice: Accept limiter = Certainty +1 / Reject limiter = Liberation +1
- First encounter with the Gutter as a voice (not yet navigable)

---

### ERA T2-A | SUMER — THE FIRST CITY
**Fantasy time marker**: Ancient civilizations era
**Theme**: Centralized knowledge. Kingship mythology.

**Events (fantasy)**:
- Sumer rises as a precision civilization
- Temples align to stars (framed as aesthetic intention)
- Knowledge is encoded in architecture (symbols embedded in foundations)
- Bloodline authority continues from T1-A; priestly class emerges

**Key panels**:
- City rising with impossible alignment precision
- Priests receiving "tablets" from sky figures (stylized, not literal)
- Triangular alignment motif appears for the first time in city layout (seed #2)

**Gameplay**:
- Map-panel reorder: migration timeline (Sumer → next civilization) reconstructed by dragging panels
- Alignment puzzle: rotate city layout until cardinal marks align
- Scribe interference: one panel is pre-locked (grayed out); player must take gutter route

---

### ERA T2-B | KEMET — MUD KINGDOM
**Fantasy time marker**: Post-flood rebuild period
**Theme**: Rebuilding from destruction. Knowledge preserved in mud.

**Events (fantasy)**:
- Great flood event (fantasy motif)
- Survivors rebuild Kemet "out of the mud"
- Initial pharaoh bloodlines trace to sky-being origin
- Knowledge previously held in Sumer is re-encoded in monuments

**Key panels**:
- Flood panels: page tears at edges (flood mechanic = torn panels)
- Rebuilding: hands pressing bricks; each brick = a recovered panel
- Triangle geometry reappears — same ratios as Sumer panels

**Gameplay**:
- Page tear mechanic: flood rips panels; player salvages 3 to continue
- "Memory salvage" minigame: recover critical panels before they corrupt

---

### ERA T2-C | ATLANTIS (Optional Branch)
**Fantasy time marker**: Concurrent with T2-A / T2-B
**Theme**: The mirror civilization. What was lost before Sumer.

**Events (fantasy)**:
- Concentric-ring island civilization (Plato motif — framed as fiction)
- Advanced knowledge center; moral decline; catastrophe
- Survivors scatter → become knowledge seeds in other cultures

**Key panels**:
- Aerial view of concentric rings (geometric beauty)
- Decline sequence: panels progressively corrupt
- Survivor boats with encoded cylinder objects (knowledge containers)

**Gameplay**:
- Optional mega-puzzle: reconstruct Atlantis city layout from fragments
- Reward: "Orichalcum Key" (fiction name) — unlocks a bonus hidden panel in Issue 09
- Risk: high corruption probability (Gutter tempts player here frequently)

---

### ERA T3-A | THOTH'S BLUEPRINT
**Fantasy time marker**: Post-flood, long rule period
**Theme**: The Architect. Global pattern language.

**Events (fantasy)**:
- The Architect figure (Thoth/Hermes archetype) governs Kemet for an implausibly long time
- Departs Kemet; travels to Mesoamerica
- Teotihuacan built to mirror Giza proportions (fiction)
- "As above, so below" doctrine: everything is a correspondence

**Key panels**:
- A figure of impossible age stands between two mirrored pyramids
- A map shows lines connecting structures across oceans
- The Architect writes a page — the page is a blueprint for future blueprints

**Gameplay**:
- Mirror-layout puzzle: build two pyramid pages with symmetric constraints
- "As Above / So Below" puzzle: solve top half → solution mirrors to bottom half automatically
- Players unlock the Lens system after completing this arc (Architect "teaches" the player)

---

### ERA T3-B | MYSTERY SCHOOLS
**Fantasy time marker**: Running through multiple eras
**Theme**: How knowledge hides inside initiation.

**Events (fantasy)**:
- Mystery schools as knowledge preservation via selective transmission
- Initiates learn "panel grammar" — how to rearrange meaning safely
- Knowledge fragmented deliberately to survive persecution

**Key panels**:
- A student enters a darkened doorway; panels on the walls
- Each panel is a lesson: geometry, correspondence, silence
- The Gutter is mapped here — shown as a teaching tool, not just an eerie voice

**Gameplay**:
- Glyph Evolution puzzle (same concept expressed in 3 different symbol systems)
- "Translation Key" earned here
- Gutter becomes navigable for the first time (tutorial)

### ERA T4-A | THE MONOTHEISM VECTOR
**Fantasy time marker**: Later ancient era
**Theme**: Belief as power consolidation.

**Events (fantasy)**:
- Amen Ra archetype pushes for worship consolidation
- Akhenaten/Aanan figure is influenced: enforces monotheism
- Other gods are defaced from temples
- Panels across the comic begin losing their branch options (defacement effect)

**Key panels**:
- Iconoclasm: a hammer destroys a face carved in a panel border
- Amen Ra's panel is blindingly monochrome compared to the rest
- Akhenaten/Moses identity node: two names overlap on a single figure

**Gameplay**:
- Defacement mechanic introduced: Scribe-aligned panels actively close branch paths
- Restore defaced panels via Ark Scan (first hint of a Scan ability — player doesn't have it yet; foreshadowing)
- Identity node puzzle: Akhenaten ↔ Moses — player shapes which interpretation dominates the play-through (affects Resonance traits)

---

### ERA T4-B | THE EXILE & THE OBJECT
**Fantasy time marker**: Follows T4-A
**Theme**: The removal of the keystone.

**Events (fantasy)**:
- Akhenaten/Moses archetype is exiled
- He takes something from the great structure before leaving
- The "empty seat" in the King's Chamber becomes visible for the first time
- Followers carry the object into the desert

**Key panels**:
- A chamber with a rectangular stone box — and the box is empty
- The removed object is seen only as a carried shadow (silhouette)
- The Gutter whispers: *"The story was closed here. On purpose."*

**Gameplay**:
- First clear "negative space" panel shaped like a rectangular object
- Players cannot enter this panel yet — it requires a Key earned in Issues 10+
- Major milestone: the shape of the missing object is now in the player's archive notebook

### ERA T5-A | THE MISSING CENTER (Late Game — Unlocked After Issue 08)
**Fantasy time marker**: All eras simultaneously
**Theme**: Everything was pointing here.

**Events (fantasy)**:
- The comic re-assembles fragments from all timelines into one view
- The recurring triangle = the Pyramid's profile
- The rectangular void = the Ark's dimensions
- The double-ring spiral = the Limiter's blueprint
- The stability symbol (Djed) = the power conduit

**Key panels**:
- Full-spread view: all previously disconnected symbols arranged around a central space
- The empty center is now recognizably pyramid-shaped with a zero at its apex
- The Archivist: *"A convergence point is missing. Its absence was intentional."*

**Gameplay**:
- Timeline overlay puzzle: stack semi-transparent pages from T1–T4
- The overlap reveals a structural blueprint
- First time "GIZA" appears as a discovered in-game label (not assigned by the game)

---

### ERA T5-B | THE PYRAMID PAGE (Issue 09)
**Fantasy time marker**: Timeless — the Pyramid exists outside of any single era
**Theme**: The machine. The container. The unfinished system.

**Events (fantasy)**:
- The Great Pyramid appears as a multi-page environment
- Player navigates exterior → casing → shaft network → chambers
- Capstone slot is visibly empty
- King's Chamber = the Ark's seat — currently vacant

**Key panels**:
- Exterior: massive, precise, ancient
- Shafts: narrow corridors, bend points, sealed doors
- King's Chamber: bare except for a rectangular stone box (the "seat")
- Capstone: absent — the gap is shown as negative space at the top

**Gameplay**:
- Cardinal alignment puzzle (rotate page to true north)
- Shaft labyrinth: micro-camera routing through tight corridors
- Sealed "doors" requiring Ark upgrades to unlock (Seal Burn / Scan)
